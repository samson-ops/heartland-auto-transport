Snowbird Car Shipping: Alabama to Florida, Arizona & Texas

Snowbird car shipping from Alabama to Florida, Arizona, and Texas is a $500 million annual industry. Tens of thousands of Alabama residents migrate south each winter — taking their cars, golf carts, and boats with them. Here's everything Alabama snowbirds need to know about seasonal car shipping: when to book, what it costs, and how to save money on round trips.

Why Alabama Snowbirds Ship Their Vehicles

Even though Alabama itself has mild winters compared to the Northeast and Midwest, plenty of Alabama residents still head south each year. The reasons vary: second homes in Gulf Coast Florida (Destin, 30A, Naples, Marco Island), winter rentals in Arizona (Scottsdale, Tucson, Phoenix), Texas Hill Country properties, or extended stays in family homes elsewhere. Shipping the car (often along with golf carts, boats, or motorcycles) is faster, less stressful, and more cost-effective than driving for most snowbirds — especially elderly residents.

The alternative is driving 8-15 hours each way, twice a year. For couples shipping two vehicles, that's 32-60 hours of windshield time across both trips. Shipping eliminates the drive and lets you fly down comfortably.

When to Book Your Snowbird Shipment

Timing is everything for snowbird shipping because the entire industry experiences the same seasonal demand spike at the same time. Southbound shipments (Alabama to Florida, Arizona, Texas) peak from mid-September through November. Carriers fill up months in advance during this window. Northbound shipments (returning to Alabama) peak from late March through May.

Book your southbound shipment 6-8 weeks in advance for the best pricing and reliable scheduling. Book your northbound shipment 4-6 weeks in advance. Last-minute snowbird shipments (within 2 weeks of pickup) often cost 25-50% more than well-planned shipments because carriers know you have no other option.

Many snowbirds book both legs (southbound in October, northbound in April) at the same time during fall, locking in pricing and guaranteed slots. This is the cheapest and most reliable approach.

Alabama to Arizona:

Round-trip booking (both legs at once) typically saves 5-10% versus booking each leg separately.

Should I Ship My Golf Cart Too?

Many snowbird destinations are golf-cart-friendly communities — The Villages, Naples retirement communities, Sun City, beach communities along 30A and Hilton Head. Shipping your golf cart is significantly cheaper than buying or renting one at your destination, especially for repeated annual trips.

Golf cart shipping from Alabama to Florida runs $300-$600 depending on cart size and destination. Multi-cart shipments (his/hers carts together) get per-cart discounts. Many snowbirds ship car + golf cart together at lower combined rates than separate shipments.

Tips to Save Money on Snowbird Shipping

Book early. This is the single biggest factor. October-booked southbound shipments can be 20% cheaper than November-booked ones for the same route.

Book both legs together. Save 5-10% versus separate bookings, and lock in your spring return slot before capacity tightens.

Be flexible on dates. A 5-7 day pickup window costs less than demanding a specific day. Snowbirds with flight flexibility have leverage to wait for carrier availability.

Combine shipments. If you have a friend, neighbor, or family member also shipping to nearby destinations, ask about coordinated pickup or drop-off. Sometimes carriers can combine loads at significant per-vehicle discounts.

Choose major-route destinations. Shipping to Naples or Phoenix directly is cheaper than rural destinations. If you're willing to drive 30 minutes from a major drop-off, you can save $100-$200.

Use open transport unless your vehicle is high-value. Most snowbird vehicles are daily-driver SUVs, sedans, or trucks. Open transport is the right choice — saves 60-80% versus enclosed.

Avoid peak weeks if you can. The week of Thanksgiving and the first week of January are especially busy. Shifting by even a week can save 10-15%.
