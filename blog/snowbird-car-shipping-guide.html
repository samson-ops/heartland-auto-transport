Snowbird Car Shipping: Alabama to Florida, Arizona & Texas Guide

Everything Alabama snowbirds need to know about shipping cars seasonally — including when to book and how to save.

Published May 2, 2026 · Heartland Auto Transport

Why Snowbirds Ship Cars (Instead of Driving)

If you're an Alabama snowbird splitting time between your primary home and a winter residence in Florida, Arizona, or Texas, you've probably faced the annual question: drive the car down (and back), or ship it? Both options have pros and cons, but shipping is increasingly popular for one big reason — it's easier on you.

Driving a car from Alabama to Florida is 10-12 hours one way. Alabama to Arizona is 24-30 hours. Alabama to Texas Hill Country is 12-15 hours. Add the wear and tear on the vehicle, fuel costs ($150-$500 each way), hotel stops, food, and your own time and energy, and shipping starts looking attractive. Most snowbirds at retirement age would rather fly down comfortably and have the car waiting at their winter home than spend two full days behind the wheel.

Heartland Auto Transport handles thousands of snowbird shipments every year between Alabama and warm-weather destinations. We know the routes, the timing patterns, and how to keep costs reasonable for repeat seasonal shippers.

Best Times to Book Snowbird Shipments

Snowbird season has predictable peaks that affect both pricing and availability.

Fall southbound (October-November): The biggest snowbird migration season. Alabama to Florida, Arizona, and Texas shipments spike from mid-October through Thanksgiving. Book 3-4 weeks in advance. Prices are elevated 15-25% above off-peak rates.

Spring northbound (March-May): The return trip season. Florida, Arizona, and Texas residents heading back to Alabama from mid-March through mid-May. Same booking advice — 3-4 weeks lead time, expect peak pricing.

Off-peak (June-September, December-February): If your schedule is flexible enough to ship during off-peak windows, prices drop and availability opens up. June through September has the absolute lowest snowbird-route pricing. December through February is also affordable if you can ship before Christmas or after New Year's.

Book early to lock in rates. Carrier capacity fills up first for popular snowbird routes. By mid-October, southbound Alabama-to-Florida routes are heavily booked. Same in March for northbound.

Snowbird Shipping Tips

Book both directions at once when possible. Many snowbirds book the southbound shipment in September and the northbound return in February or early March. Locking in both directions ahead of peak season ensures availability and may earn you a small loyalty discount.

Coordinate with your second-home logistics. If you're shipping to a condo or HOA-managed community, check whether the property has restrictions on commercial vehicle access. Some retirement communities and resorts require coordination with property management for tractor-trailer delivery. We handle this coordination for you, but it requires advance notice.

Consider shipping your second vehicle too. Many snowbird couples ship two vehicles — one daily driver and one classic, sports car, or golf cart. Multi-vehicle shipments to the same destination get per-vehicle discounts. Add a golf cart shipment in the same trailer for significant savings.

Keep your vehicle's documents accessible. Have registration, insurance card, and one set of keys ready for the driver. The Bill of Lading is the most important document of the entire process — make sure you sign it carefully at both pickup and delivery.

Plan for the timing gap. Auto transport takes 3-7 days for snowbird routes. If you're flying down before your car arrives, plan for ground transportation at your destination — taxi, ride-share, or rental car — until your vehicle is delivered.

Snowbird-Specific Concerns

What if I'm shipping a classic or luxury car? Many snowbirds own restored classics or luxury vehicles they keep at their winter homes. For these vehicles, enclosed transport is recommended — see our enclosed auto transport service for details.

Will my car battery die during transit? Modern vehicle batteries can sit unused for 7-10 days without dying. For older vehicles or extended storage at your second home, consider a battery tender or battery disconnect.

What about my golf cart? Snowbirds in golf cart-friendly communities (The Villages, Sun City, beach communities along 30A) often ship their cart along with their car. See our golf cart shipping page for details. Multi-vehicle shipments are significantly cheaper per vehicle.

Can I leave personal items in my car? Most carriers allow up to 100 lbs of personal items in the trunk below the window line. Useful for snowbirds — you can ship season-specific clothing, beach gear, golf clubs, etc. with your vehicle. Just don't ship anything irreplaceable (cargo insurance doesn't cover personal items).
