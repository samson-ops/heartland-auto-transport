How Auto Transport Works at Heartland

Shipping a vehicle should be straightforward. From the moment you request a quote to the moment your keys are back in your hand, we make every step transparent, honest, and easy. Here's exactly what happens when you ship with us.

The 5-Step Process

From quote to delivery, the entire process takes 1-10 days depending on distance. Here's how we make it happen.

1

Request a Free Quote

Tell us where your vehicle is, where it's going, what kind of vehicle it is, and when you want it picked up. Submit online in under 60 seconds, or call (888) 123-4567 to speak directly with our Alabama-based team. You'll receive a transparent, all-in quote within minutes — no email gatekeeping, no high-pressure sales, no hidden fees revealed later.

What we need: Pickup address, delivery address, vehicle year/make/model, your preferred pickup date, and whether your vehicle is operable.

2

Book Your Shipment

If the quote works for you, accept it and provide a small deposit (typically $100-$200, applied toward your total). Once booked, your shipment is assigned to a dedicated dispatcher who handles everything from here. We match your vehicle with one of our vetted carrier partners whose route aligns with your timing and locations.

Every carrier in our network is verified: active FMCSA authority, current cargo insurance, and clean safety records. We don't dispatch your vehicle to cheap carriers that cut corners.

3

Vehicle Pickup

The assigned driver contacts you 24 hours before pickup to confirm an arrival window. At pickup, you (or a designated representative 18+ with photo ID) meet the driver at your address. Together, you inspect the vehicle and document its current condition. Both parties sign a Bill of Lading — the legal document recording the vehicle's exact condition at pickup.

Door-to-door service: Our drivers come to your home, business, or any Alabama address. There's no driving to terminals, no waiting at freight depots. If a tractor-trailer can't legally access your street, we coordinate a nearby meet-up location.

4

In-Transit Updates

Your vehicle is loaded onto the carrier and secured with industry-standard wheel chocks and soft straps. Your dispatcher keeps you informed throughout the journey. You can call, text, or email anytime for status updates — there's no rotating call center queue, just your dedicated point of contact.

Typical transit times: In-state Alabama moves complete in 1-3 days. Regional moves to neighboring Southern states take 2-5 days. Cross-country shipments to the West Coast or Northeast take 7-10 days.

5

Delivery

The driver contacts you before arrival at the destination. You meet at the agreed delivery location, inspect the vehicle thoroughly, and sign the final Bill of Lading confirming it arrived in the same condition it left. The keys are back in your hand, and your shipment is complete.

Payment: The remaining balance is collected on delivery (typically by cashier's check or cash to the driver), unless you've prepaid in full upfront. No surprise charges, no fees revealed at delivery — the quote you accepted is the final price.

How Long Does Auto Transport Take?

The biggest factor in transit time is distance, but it's not the only one. Here are realistic transit windows from Alabama:

  • In-state Alabama (Birmingham ↔ Mobile, Huntsville ↔ Montgomery): 1-2 days from pickup to delivery
  • Regional Southern routes (Alabama to Georgia, Florida, Tennessee, Mississippi): 2-4 days
  • Mid-distance (Alabama to Texas, Carolinas, Virginia, Ohio): 3-6 days
  • Cross-country (Alabama to California, Pacific Northwest, Northeast): 7-10 days

From the time you book to the time your driver arrives for pickup is typically 1-5 days. Combined, expect 3-15 days from booking to final delivery for most shipments. Expedited shipping can compress these timelines for time-sensitive moves.

How Much Does Auto Transport Cost?

Auto transport cost depends on six main factors: distance (longer = more dollars total, less per mile), vehicle size and weight (full-size SUVs and trucks cost more than compact cars), route popularity (major corridors are cheaper), time of year (summer is peak, January-February are cheapest), pickup flexibility (5-7 day windows get the best rates), and transport type (open is cheapest, enclosed costs 60-80% more).

Realistic price ranges: short regional routes start around $400-$700, mid-distance shipments run $700-$1,200, and cross-country transport ranges $1,000-$1,800 for standard vehicles. Luxury and exotic vehicles in enclosed transport cost more.

How to Prepare Your Vehicle

A little prep makes shipping smoother. Wash your vehicle before pickup so pre-existing scratches and dings are clearly visible during inspection. Reduce gas to a quarter tank — full tanks add unnecessary weight. Remove all personal items above the window line (most carriers allow up to 100 lbs in the trunk). Disable any aftermarket alarms. Fold mirrors in and retract the antenna if possible. Provide a key (and ideally a backup key). Document the vehicle's condition with photos from multiple angles. Note any mechanical issues — non-running vehicles need a winch-equipped carrier.

What If Something Goes Wrong?

Auto transport damage is rare with vetted carriers — well under 1% of shipments — but if anything happens, you're protected. Every carrier in our network carries active cargo insurance. If damage occurs, document it on the Bill of Lading at delivery, take photos, and contact us immediately. Your dispatcher walks you through the claim process with the carrier's insurance company. We don't disappear after delivery — we're here until any issue is fully resolved.
