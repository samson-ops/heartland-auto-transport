Original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) operate at a scale where consumer-grade auto transport isn't sufficient. Production lines turn out hundreds of vehicles daily, factory pools fill quickly, and franchise dealer networks need consistent inbound flow to maintain inventory. Heartland Auto Transport provides supplemental OEM vehicle shipping that integrates with manufacturer logistics — handling overflow capacity, specialty moves, and dealer-network shipments that fall outside primary carrier contracts.

What Heartland's OEM Vehicle Shipping Includes

Factory-to-Dealer Transport

When primary OEM carriers are at capacity or specific routes need supplemental coverage, we step in. Common scenarios include model-year launch surges, regional dealer network gaps, specialty trim or limited-edition shipments, and emergency reroutes.

Pre-Production & Engineering Vehicles

OEMs ship pre-production prototypes, engineering validation units, press fleet vehicles, and certification fleet vehicles to test facilities, dealer events, photoshoots, and journalist locations. These shipments often require enclosed transport, specific timing, and confidentiality. We handle these moves with the discretion OEM marketing and engineering teams require.

Ramp-Up Logistics Support

New model launches and capacity expansions create temporary spikes in transport demand. Manufacturer fleet contracts may not flex fast enough to handle these spikes — we provide the supplemental capacity to bridge the gap during ramp-up periods.

OEM-Aligned Reporting

OEM logistics teams need detailed reporting on every shipment: VINs, origin, destination, transit time, condition at delivery. We provide manufacturer-grade documentation that integrates with OEM logistics systems and accounting requirements.

  • Overflow capacity during model-year launch and ramp-up periods
  • Pre-production transport for engineering, marketing, and certification vehicles
  • Press fleet logistics — moving journalist vehicles between media events and reviewers

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a oem vehicle shipping shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does oem vehicle shipping cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
