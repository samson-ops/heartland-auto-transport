What Heartland's Dealer Auto Transport for Dealerships Includes

Dealer Trade Coordination

Dealer-to-dealer trades happen constantly in the franchise world — a customer wants a specific color, trim, or option package, and the dealer locates one at a sister store hundreds of miles away. We coordinate these trades with both sides of the transaction, handle pickup and delivery on the dealer's schedule, and provide tracking visibility for both buying and selling dealers throughout transit.

Auction Pickup Service

Manheim, Adesa, ACV, and independent auction yards have strict pickup deadlines — typically 5-7 days after sale, with daily storage fees after. We dispatch carriers to auction yards quickly, handle the gate paperwork and release procedures, and deliver vehicles directly to your dealership or detail facility.

Wholesale Dealer Pricing

Dealer rates differ from retail rates. Dealers shipping multiple vehicles per month qualify for volume pricing, dedicated dispatcher relationships, and streamlined billing. Whether you ship 5 vehicles a month or 50, we'll work out pricing that reflects your actual volume.

Lot Replenishment

New car dealers receive factory deliveries on transporters, but used car lots often need ongoing replenishment from auctions, trade-ins from sister stores, and inventory acquired across state lines. We handle these regular shipments with predictable scheduling so your inventory pipeline stays full.

Specific Services Offered

  • Open transport for standard inventory — the most cost-effective option for the vast majority of dealer trades and auction pickups

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a dealer auto transport shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does dealer auto transport cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
