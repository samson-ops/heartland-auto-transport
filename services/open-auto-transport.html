How Our Open Carrier Transport Works

Shipping a vehicle with Heartland follows a clear, documented process that protects you at every stage. Here's what to expect from booking through delivery.

1

Step 1: Free Quote and Booking

Provide your pickup and delivery locations, vehicle make, model, year, and vehicle size, whether it's operable, your preferred dates, and your desired transport method — open and enclosed transport options are both available. Alabama auto transport typically costs $0.60–$1.75 per mile, with total prices between $500–$1,400 depending on distance, vehicle size, and service tier. We calculate your car shipping quote instantly so you can make an informed decision.

2

Step 2: Vehicle Pickup and Loading

On pickup day, the carrier conducts a thorough pre-transport inspection documented on a Bill of Lading — every scratch, dent, and blemish is recorded so both parties have a clear condition baseline. Your vehicle is then driven or loaded onto the open trailer and secured following industry best practices. If your vehicle is inoperable, a winch is used to load it safely, though an additional fee of approximately $150–$250 applies for the extra equipment and labor involved. This documentation process is supported by both FMCSA regulations and Alabama state law.

3

Step 3: Transport and Delivery

Once loaded, your car shipment moves along its route with scheduled driver rest stops, fueling, and compliance with hours-of-service rules. You receive tracking updates throughout transit. Upon arrival, you inspect the vehicle against the original condition report before signing the delivery receipt. Any discrepancies are documented immediately, which supports the carrier's cargo insurance claim process if needed. Payment is typically due upon delivery, and the whole process concludes with your car arrived safely at its destination.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does open carrier auto transport cost in Alabama?
Open car transport cost depends on distance, vehicle size, route demand, and time of year. For regional Alabama shipments under 500 miles, expect $400–$650 for an open carrier. Neighboring-state moves to Georgia, Tennessee, Mississippi, or Florida typically cost $350–$475 for a standard sedan. Cross-country shipments to California average $1,250–$1,800. Most open carrier costs in Alabama run between $0.60 and $1.05 per mile on southeast corridors. Open transport can save you $200–$500 compared to enclosed transport, making open car shipping affordable for the majority of vehicle shipping needs. Expedited or rush service adds 20–40% above standard rates.
How long does vehicle transport take within Alabama?
Transit times depend on distance: under 200 miles takes 1–2 days, 200–500 miles takes 2–3 days, 500–1,000 miles takes 3–5 days, and cross-country routes over 1,500 miles typically take 6–10 days. Pickup windows along major corridors like I-65 average 2–4 business days, while rural delivery locations may require 4–7 business days. Seasonal demand, weather events, and driver availability all influence scheduling.
Is open auto transport safe for my vehicle in Alabama?
Yes — 95% of vehicles can be safely transported on open carriers, and most new vehicles are shipped on open carriers from manufacturers to dealerships using this exact method. Open transport exposes vehicles to weather elements and road debris, which can occasionally cause minor cosmetic wear like small paint chips. However, all carriers in Heartland's network maintain FMCSA-required insurance coverage, and Alabama state law requires carriers to carry both cargo and liability insurance. For car enthusiasts shipping a classic car or luxury cars requiring maximum protection, enclosed transport provides extra protection from wind, rain, and debris — though enclosed carriers typically offer higher insurance coverage than open carriers and cost 40–60% more. Alabama's large-truck safety environment has also improved significantly, with fatal crashes involving large trucks and buses declining from approximately 139 in 2021 to 23 by 2025.
What should I do to prepare my vehicle for open carrier transport?
Before your pickup date, remove all personal belongings from the vehicle — passengers are at risk of theft or vandalism with open transport, and personal items are not covered by the carrier's cargo insurance. Wash the vehicle so pre-existing damage is visible during the condition inspection. Ensure the gas tank is no more than a quarter full to reduce weight. Disable aftermarket alarms, retract antennas, and secure or remove loose accessories. If the vehicle is inoperable, inform us at booking so we can arrange proper loading equipment. Have your keys available and be present (or designate someone) for the pre-pickup inspection and Bill of Lading documentation.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open trailer shipping uses multi-level open air carriers that can carry 8–10 vehicles at once, making it the most common car shipping method and the most budget-friendly option. Enclosed auto transport uses a sealed enclosed trailer that shields vehicles from all weather and road debris. Enclosed transport is ideal for luxury and classic cars, heavily modified vehicles, or any car where maximum protection justifies the 40–60% cost premium. Open transport offers greater flexibility for larger vehicles and is more readily available than enclosed transport due to the sheer number of open transport carriers operating nationwide. For most standard vehicles, open carrier car transport provides the right balance of safety, cost, and scheduling convenience.
