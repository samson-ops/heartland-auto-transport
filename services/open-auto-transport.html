Common Questions

Open Carrier Transport, Answered

How much does open carrier auto transport cost in Alabama?
Cost depends on distance, vehicle size, route demand, and time of year. For regional Alabama shipments under 500 miles, expect roughly $400–$700. Mid-distance routes run $700–$1,100, and cross-country shipments $1,100–$1,600 on open transport. Get an exact quote using the form above.
How long does vehicle transport take within Alabama?
Transit times depend on distance: under 200 miles takes 1–2 days, 200–500 miles takes 2–3 days, 500–1,000 miles takes 3–5 days, and cross-country routes take 7–10 days.
Is open auto transport safe for my vehicle?
Yes. About 95% of vehicles can be safely transported on open carriers — it's the same method manufacturers use to ship new vehicles to dealerships. Your car is securely strapped and fully insured throughout transit.
What should I do to prepare my vehicle?
Before pickup, remove all personal belongings (they're not covered and add theft risk on open transport), leave about a quarter tank of fuel, disable toll tags and alarms, and note any existing damage. Wash the car so the inspection is accurate.
What's the difference between open and enclosed transport?
Open transport uses multi-level carriers that haul 8–10 vehicles at once — the most common and most affordable method. Enclosed transport ships your vehicle inside a covered trailer for maximum protection, costing 60–80% more. Open is ideal for standard vehicles; enclosed suits luxury, classic, and exotic cars.
