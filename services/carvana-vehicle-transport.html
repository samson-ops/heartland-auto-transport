What Heartland's Carvana Vehicle Transport Includes

When Heartland Beats Carvana Delivery

Heartland makes sense when (1) Carvana's delivery timeline is too slow for your schedule, (2) the vehicle is at a distant hub and Carvana wants to relocate it first (adding days or weeks), (3) you want a guaranteed delivery date instead of Carvana's flexible window, or (4) you prefer direct communication with one provider throughout the process.

Hub Pickup Coordination

We coordinate with the Carvana hub where your vehicle is stored. You may need to release the vehicle for our pickup (Carvana typically allows this once the sale is finalized). Once cleared, we dispatch a driver to the hub, complete inspection paperwork, and load.

Faster Cross-Country Delivery

Carvana sometimes routes vehicles cross-country through their internal logistics network, which can take 2-4 weeks. We dispatch carriers directly from the hub on standard auto transport timelines — typically 7-10 days for cross-country moves, with no intermediate stops.

Direct Driver Inspection

When Carvana delivers, the driver is a Carvana-contracted employee with their own inspection process. With Heartland, you do the inspection alongside a Heartland-network driver who walks through condition documentation with you using standard auto transport Bills of Lading.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a carvana vehicle transport shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does carvana vehicle transport cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
