What Heartland's Freight Shipping Includes

Full Truckload (FTL)

Full truckload service dedicates an entire trailer to your shipment. The truck goes from origin to destination without consolidation, making FTL faster than LTL for large shipments and ideal when freight needs to stay together or arrive on tight timelines.

Less-Than-Truckload (LTL)

LTL shipping moves smaller freight (typically 150-15,000 pounds) by consolidating multiple customers' freight on a single carrier. LTL pricing is per-pound or per-pallet, making it cost-effective for shipments too small to warrant a dedicated truck.

Expedited Freight

When standard transit times aren't fast enough, expedited freight prioritizes your shipment with dedicated routing and faster transit. Premium pricing applies, but the time savings often justify it for urgent freight.

Specialty Freight

Beyond standard freight, specialty categories include refrigerated (reefer), temperature-controlled, hazardous materials (hazmat), high-value goods, and oversize cargo. Each requires specialized carriers and handling. We coordinate the right specialty service for each load.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a freight shipping shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does freight shipping cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
