IAAI (Insurance Auto Auctions, now branded IAA) is the second-largest salvage auction network in the United States, with yards across the country. Buyers — dealers, rebuilders, exporters, and retail customers — purchase vehicles online and need fast pickup coordination. Heartland Auto Transport dispatches to IAA yards quickly, handles the salvage logistics, and delivers nationwide.

What Heartland's IAAI Vehicle Shipping Includes

IAA Network Coverage

We pick up from any IAA yard nationwide — from major metropolitan facilities to smaller regional yards. Our drivers know IAA gate procedures, hours, and release requirements.

Storage Fee Avoidance

IAA charges daily storage fees after the standard pickup window expires. These accumulate quickly — a typical $30-40/day fee means a two-week delay adds $400+ to your effective cost. We dispatch fast to keep that exposure low.

Non-Running & Damaged Vehicles

Most IAA inventory comes from insurance total-loss claims, meaning damaged condition is the norm. Standard auto carriers often can't handle these vehicles. Our network includes winch-equipped flatbeds and rollbacks designed for damaged or non-running pickups.

Title & Documentation

IAA vehicles come with various title brands — clean, salvage, certificate of destruction, parts-only. Our paperwork accommodates each type, and we don't pick up vehicles where the title situation isn't clear at our end.

  • Drivable vehicle transport on standard open carriers for cosmetic-damage units

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a iaai vehicle shipping shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does iaai vehicle shipping cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
