Why EV Shipping Needs Specialized Knowledge

Electric vehicle shipping is the fastest-growing segment of auto transport, and it requires understanding that goes beyond loading a car onto a trailer. EVs are heavier than equivalent gasoline vehicles — sometimes by 1,000 pounds or more — because of their massive battery packs. A Tesla Model Y weighs about 4,400 lbs versus 3,800 for a comparable BMW X3. A Rivian R1S weighs nearly 7,000 lbs. This added weight affects how the carrier is loaded, which deck position the vehicle occupies, and total carrier weight limits.

Beyond weight, EVs have unique pre-transport considerations: state of charge, regenerative braking systems that engage when wheels turn, and 12V battery management. Heartland Auto Transport's carriers understand these nuances. How to Prepare Your EV for Transport

Preparing an EV for shipping is slightly different from preparing a gasoline vehicle.
  • Charge to about 50%: Don't ship with a fully charged battery (some recommend less for safety) or a near-empty battery (which could die during transit). 40-60% state of charge is ideal.
  • Disable sentry mode and dashcam features: These drain the 12V battery during transport. Tesla owners specifically should turn off Sentry Mode in the app.
  • Provide the access card or key fob: The driver needs to be able to put the vehicle in transport/tow mode for loading.
  • Note any transport-specific modes: Tesla has a Transport Mode. Rivian has a similar feature. Tell us if you've activated these.
  • Document the vehicle's condition: Photos from every angle, including the underbody if possible — EVs have exposed battery packs that are critical to inspect.
  • Disable any aftermarket alarms: Same as any other vehicle.

EV Shipping Cost — More Than Standard Vehicles

EV shipping typically costs 10-15% more than equivalent gasoline vehicle shipping for a few reasons: EVs are heavier, EVs take up the same deck space but reduce total carrier weight capacity, and some carriers charge a small EV surcharge to account for additional handling complexity. Open vs. Enclosed Transport for EVs

Most daily-driver EVs ship safely on open carriers, just like gasoline vehicles. Tesla Model 3s, Mustang Mach-Es, Chevy Bolts, and similar mainstream EVs are fine on open transport. For high-end EVs like the Lucid Air Sapphire, Tesla Model S Plaid, Rivian R1S Launch Edition, Mercedes EQS, Porsche Taycan Turbo S, or any Cybertruck, we recommend our enclosed transport service to protect the vehicle's finish and high-tech sensors from road debris.

EV-Specific Concerns We Handle

Customers frequently ask about specific EV transport concerns. Battery discharge during transport: minimal — modern EVs lose only 1-3% per week when properly prepared. Regenerative braking during loading: not an issue when the vehicle is in transport/tow mode. Damage to charging ports: very rare, but our carriers know to avoid contact with the charging port area during securement. Insurance for high-value EVs: every shipment is covered by the carrier's cargo insurance, which adequately covers most EVs. For ultra-high-value EVs (Lucid Air Sapphire, Tesla Model S Plaid, six-figure trims), we can arrange additional supplemental coverage on request.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does shipping an EV cost more than shipping a regular car?

Yes, typically 10-15% more. EVs are heavier (sometimes by 1,000+ lbs) and reduce total carrier weight capacity, which means slightly higher per-vehicle costs. The exact premium varies by EV model and route.

Should I ship my Tesla or EV with a full battery?

No. Ship with 40-60% state of charge. Full batteries are heavier than necessary and some carriers have safety preferences around high SOC. Empty batteries risk dying during transit. 50% is the sweet spot.

Can you ship a Tesla Cybertruck?

Yes — Cybertruck shipping is now routine. The Cybertruck's weight (~6,600 lbs) and size require specific carrier matching, and we recommend enclosed transport to protect the stainless-steel finish from road debris and minor dings.

Will my EV's range be reduced after shipping?

No. Shipping has zero permanent impact on EV range or battery health. You may notice 1-3% battery drain over a week of transit due to onboard electronics, but that recovers fully on the next charge.

Do EV transport carriers know how to handle EVs?

Our network includes carriers specifically experienced with EVs. They know how to engage transport/tow mode, avoid charging port areas during securement, and handle the heavier weight properly. We don't dispatch EVs to inexperienced carriers.

Can you ship an EV with a non-working battery?

Yes, but it requires a carrier with a winch since the wheels won't roll under EV-style regenerative braking if the 12V battery is dead. We handle salvage EVs, totaled EVs being shipped to repair shops, and out-of-warranty battery replacements.
