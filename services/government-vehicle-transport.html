Government fleets — federal agencies, state DOTs, county sheriffs, municipal police, and public works departments — operate under specific procurement procedures, documentation requirements, and accountability standards. Heartland Auto Transport handles government vehicle shipping with the paperwork, insurance, and reporting that procurement officers and fleet managers need to satisfy oversight requirements.

What Heartland's Government Vehicle Transport Includes

Federal Agency Transport

Federal agencies regularly relocate vehicles between offices, regional motor pools, and end-of-service auction destinations. We handle these moves with documentation appropriate for federal accountability — VINs, condition reports, signed Bills of Lading, and final delivery confirmations.

State & Municipal Fleets

State motor pools, DOT fleets, sheriff and police department vehicles, and municipal works trucks all move regularly. We dispatch carriers for individual unit moves, multi-vehicle redeployments, and end-of-life remarketing transport.

Procurement-Compliant Documentation

Government procurement requires specific documentation: certificates of insurance, FMCSA carrier authority verification, bonding confirmation, and detailed shipment records. We provide this documentation at the procurement stage so government buyers can satisfy compliance requirements quickly.

Specialty Government Vehicles

Police interceptors, ambulances, fire department vehicles, public works trucks, and specialty government vehicles often have unique equipment, markings, or operational status that affect shipping. We coordinate appropriate carriers and document handling for these specialty units.

Specific Services Offered

  • Federal fleet vehicle transport — moves between federal facilities and to remarketing destinations
  • State DOT & motor pool transport — vehicle redistribution across state motor pool locations
  • Police, sheriff, and fire department vehicle shipping — patrol cars, interceptors, command vehicles
  • Municipal works fleet transport — utility trucks, public works vehicles, parks and recreation vehicles
  • Government auction transport — pickup and delivery from federal and state surplus auction sites

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a government vehicle transport shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does government vehicle transport cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
