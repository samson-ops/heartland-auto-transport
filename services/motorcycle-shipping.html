Motorcycle Shipping Requires Specialized Care

Motorcycle shipping is fundamentally different from car shipping. Bikes are smaller, lighter, and far more vulnerable to damage if not properly secured. A single tipover can cause thousands of dollars in fairing, paint, and exhaust damage. That's why Heartland Auto Transport treats motorcycle shipping as a specialty service — not just an afterthought to car transport.

When you ship a motorcycle with us, we match you with carriers experienced in motorcycle transport. That means proper wheel chocks designed for motorcycles, soft straps that don't damage handlebars or controls, and either an enclosed trailer (preferred) or a covered section of an open carrier. We ship Harley-Davidson cruisers and baggers, Honda Gold Wings and adventure bikes, BMW R-series and GS models, Ducati and MV Agusta exotics, Indian touring bikes, sport bikes (R1, GSX-R, ZX-10R, CBR, Ninja, Panigale), dirt bikes and motocross bikes, vintage and classic motorcycles, and trikes and trike conversions.

Enclosed vs. Open Motorcycle Transport

We strongly recommend enclosed motorcycle transport for most shipments. Enclosed trailers protect your bike from road debris, weather, and theft. The cost difference is usually $100-$300 compared to open transport, which is a small premium given the value most motorcycles carry. We use carriers with dedicated motorcycle sections in their enclosed trailers — your bike is secured upright with wheel chocks and tied down with soft straps, never crammed into a standard car-shipping configuration.

For budget-conscious shipping of less valuable motorcycles, open transport is available. Your bike will be secured upright with proper wheel chocks on the lower deck of a multi-car carrier. While it's exposed to weather, the risk of damage is low with experienced carriers.

How to Prepare Your Motorcycle for Shipping

  • Drain the gas tank to a quarter or less. Less fuel reduces weight and fire risk.
  • Disable the alarm or anti-theft system.
  • Document the bike's condition with detailed photos from all angles, including any existing scratches, dings, or paint chips.
  • Remove or secure any loose accessories — saddlebags, windshields, GPS units, helmets, gear bags. Loose items can shift and cause damage.
  • Fold mirrors in if possible.
  • Check that tire pressure is correct so the bike rolls smoothly during loading.
  • Provide a second key if you have one.
  • For non-running motorcycles, tell us upfront so we send a carrier with a ramp.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does motorcycle shipping cost?

Motorcycle shipping from Alabama typically costs $350 to $1,800 depending on distance and whether you choose enclosed or open transport. Enclosed transport (recommended) costs $100-$300 more than open transport but provides significantly better protection.

Should I ship my motorcycle enclosed or open?

We strongly recommend enclosed shipping for most motorcycles. The cost difference is small relative to the protection from weather, road debris, and theft. Open transport is acceptable for less-valuable utility bikes or short-distance moves.

Can you ship a non-running motorcycle?

Yes. Inoperable motorcycles require a carrier with a ramp or lift. There's typically a small surcharge. Tell us upfront when you request a quote so we match you with the right carrier.

How is my motorcycle secured during transport?

Motorcycles are loaded into wheel chocks designed for bikes, then secured with soft straps over the handlebars and frame — never the fairings or controls. The bike stays upright the entire journey. Properly secured motorcycles experience virtually no movement during transit.

Do you ship motorcycles to and from motorcycle events?

Yes, we ship to and from Sturgis, Daytona Bike Week, Laconia Motorcycle Week, Americade, the Tail of the Dragon, and other major motorcycle destinations. Book early during peak seasons — capacity fills up months in advance for events like Sturgis.
