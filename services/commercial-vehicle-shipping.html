Commercial vehicles aren't passenger cars, and shipping them isn't quite the same. Work trucks loaded with toolboxes and ladder racks, sprinter vans configured for specific service businesses, box trucks at oversize specifications, and refrigerated units with operating equipment — each has unique dimensions, weight, and handling needs. Heartland Auto Transport ships commercial vehicles for tradespeople, service companies, contractors, fleet operators, and commercial vehicle resellers nationwide.

What Heartland's Commercial Vehicle Shipping Includes

Work Truck Transport

Plumbing trucks, HVAC service vans, electrician vans, contractor pickups with tool boxes and ladder racks — all routinely shipped without removing the work-related equipment. We coordinate the right carrier for the truck's actual loaded dimensions and weight.

Sprinter & Cargo Van Shipping

Mercedes Sprinter, Ford Transit, Ram ProMaster, and Nissan NV vans are common service vehicles. We ship them empty or with secured contents (depending on cargo type), handling the longer wheelbase and taller height compared to standard passenger vans.

Box Truck & Refrigerated Shipping

Box trucks and refrigerated units require carriers that can handle the box dimensions — typically 16-26 feet long and 8 feet tall. Refrigerated units may require operating during transport to maintain temperature for cargo. We arrange appropriate carriers and handle special requirements.

Commercial Insurance Coverage

Commercial vehicles often exceed standard cargo insurance limits. We arrange supplemental insurance coverage for high-value commercial units (specialty service vehicles, custom-built work trucks, refrigerated units with cargo). Coverage limits scale to the vehicle and contents value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a commercial vehicle shipping shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does commercial vehicle shipping cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
