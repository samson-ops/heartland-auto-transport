What Is Enclosed Auto Transport?

Enclosed auto transport ships your vehicle inside a fully enclosed trailer rather than on an open multi-car carrier. The trailer's walls, roof, and rear doors completely shield your car from weather, road debris, dust, UV exposure, and prying eyes during the entire journey.

This is the same method used to ship cars to Barrett-Jackson auctions, RM Sotheby's events, exotic dealerships, and high-end private collections. If you own a vehicle that's irreplaceable, valuable, or simply too special for standard open transport, enclosed shipping is the right call.

When Enclosed Transport Is Worth the Premium

Enclosed transport costs 30-60% more than open transport, but for a vehicle worth $75,000, $300,000, or one of one — that premium is insurance against irreplaceable damage. If your vehicle's value exceeds $50,000, or it has sentimental value that can't be measured in dollars, enclosed is the smart choice.

What Makes Enclosed Transport Different

Weather Protection

No rain, snow, hail, salt spray, sun damage, or road debris will touch your vehicle during transit.

Lift-Gate Loading

Hydraulic lift-gates eliminate the risk of low-clearance damage when loading lowered vehicles or cars with aggressive aero kits.

Soft-Tie Strapping

Premium soft straps secure your vehicle through the wheels rather than over the body — no risk of paint damage or undercarriage stress.

Higher Insurance Limits

Enclosed carriers typically carry $250,000 to $1,000,000+ in cargo insurance — necessary for high-value vehicles.

Who Needs Enclosed Auto Transport

Enclosed transport isn't necessary for every vehicle, but for certain owners and certain cars, it's the only acceptable option. Consider enclosed shipping if you're transporting:

Classic and Antique Vehicles

Pre-1980 classics, restored muscle cars, original condition survivors, and any car where original paint and patina matter. A small stone chip on a 1969 Camaro that's been preserved for 50 years is a tragedy. Enclosed transport prevents that.

Exotic and Supercar

Ferrari, Lamborghini, McLaren, Porsche 911 GT3, Aston Martin, Bugatti — these vehicles often have aggressive aero kits, low ground clearance, and finishes that don't tolerate road salt or stone chips. Lift-gate loading and enclosed trailers protect them properly.

Luxury Vehicles

Rolls-Royce, Bentley, high-end Mercedes-Benz, BMW M-series, Cadillac Escalade Platinum, and other premium vehicles where pristine appearance matters and the resale impact of even minor cosmetic damage is significant.

Show Cars and Concours Vehicles

If you're shipping to or from a Pebble Beach, Amelia Island, Cars and Coffee, or any concours event, enclosed transport keeps your vehicle in show-ready condition. Many event venues require enclosed transport for entries.

Custom Builds and Hot Rods

Custom paint jobs, airbrush work, restomod builds, and one-off creations are nearly impossible to replicate if damaged. Enclosed transport is the only sensible option.

Lowered, Modified, or Track Vehicles

Stanced cars, dropped trucks, race cars with aero kits, and vehicles with non-stock ground clearance benefit enormously from lift-gate loading available on enclosed trailers.

Enclosed Transport Options

Not all enclosed transport is the same. We offer two levels:

Standard Enclosed (Multi-Car)

A fully enclosed trailer carrying 4-7 vehicles. More affordable than single-car transport, still provides complete protection from weather and road debris. Most customers choose this option — it delivers 95% of the benefit at a more accessible price point.

Single-Car Enclosed (White-Glove)

Your vehicle is the only car in the trailer. Used for ultra-high-value exotics, museum pieces, and situations requiring the absolute maximum care. More expensive but offers the highest level of service, including potential same-day or expedited timing.

What Enclosed Auto Transport Costs

Expect to pay 30-60% more for enclosed transport compared to open transport on the same route. As a general guide:

  • Regional moves (under 500 miles): $700-$1,200
  • Mid-distance (500-1,500 miles): $1,200-$1,800
  • Cross-country (2,000+ miles): $1,800-$3,000+
  • Single-car enclosed: Add 50-100% to multi-car enclosed pricing

Pricing varies by route, season, vehicle size, and whether the vehicle is operational. For an accurate quote on your specific shipment, get a free instant quote or call us directly.

The Heartland Enclosed Transport Process

When you ship with us, here's what happens:

  1. Quote and book: Get a transparent quote, then secure your spot with a small deposit.
  2. Carrier assignment: We match you with a carrier from our vetted enclosed network — drivers we know personally and trust with high-value vehicles.
  3. Pickup coordination: Your driver contacts you 24-48 hours before pickup. We coordinate timing windows that work for your schedule.
  4. Inspection and load: At pickup, you and the driver complete a detailed condition report with photos. The vehicle is loaded via lift-gate (if applicable) and secured with soft straps.
  5. In-transit communication: You'll get updates from your dispatcher and direct access to the driver during transit.
  6. Delivery and inspection: At delivery, you inspect the vehicle, compare to the pickup condition report, sign the Bill of Lading, and the keys are yours.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much more does enclosed transport cost compared to open?

Enclosed transport typically costs 30-60% more than open transport on the same route. For example, a $1,000 open shipment might cost $1,300-$1,600 enclosed. The premium reflects lower carrier capacity (4-7 cars per trailer instead of 8-10), higher insurance, and specialized equipment.

Is enclosed transport worth it for my car?

If your vehicle's value exceeds $50,000, is a classic, exotic, or has irreplaceable sentimental value, enclosed transport is worth the premium. For daily drivers and standard vehicles, open transport delivers the same outcome at much lower cost.

Do enclosed carriers have lift-gates?

Most professional enclosed carriers have hydraulic lift-gates that eliminate the risk of low-clearance damage when loading lowered, modified, or aero-kitted vehicles. Confirm lift-gate availability when booking if your vehicle is particularly low.

How long does enclosed auto transport take?

Enclosed transport timing is similar to open transport — 1-3 days for in-state Alabama moves, 2-5 days regional, and 7-10 days cross-country. However, because enclosed carriers are less common than open ones, pickup scheduling can take 3-7 days versus 1-3 for open.

What insurance do enclosed carriers carry?

Reputable enclosed carriers carry $250,000 to $1,000,000+ in cargo insurance, significantly higher than typical open carrier coverage. Insurance certificates are provided before pickup. For ultra-high-value vehicles, we can verify insurance limits in advance.

Can I track my vehicle during enclosed transport?

Yes. You'll receive updates from your dispatcher throughout transit and have direct phone access to your driver. Many of our enclosed carriers also offer real-time GPS tracking on request.
