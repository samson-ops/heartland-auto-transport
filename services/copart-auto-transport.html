Copart operates the largest network of salvage and clean-title auctions in the United States, with over 200 yards across the country. Heartland Auto Transport dispatches to Copart yards fast, handles the salvage-specific logistics (winches, non-running vehicles, missing keys), and delivers to wherever you need the vehicle.

What Heartland's Copart Auto Transport Includes

Storage Fee Urgency

Copart's standard pickup window is typically 3-5 business days after sale. After that, daily storage fees accumulate. A two-week delay can add $300-$500 to your effective cost. We dispatch quickly to minimize storage exposure.

Salvage & Non-Running Vehicles

Most Copart vehicles have damage, may not start, or are missing keys. Standard auto carriers can't always handle these conditions. Our network includes winch-equipped flatbed and rollback carriers for non-running pickups, plus drivers experienced with damaged-vehicle loading.

Gate Procedure

Copart yards have specific release procedures: gate pass, member ID verification, payment confirmation. Our drivers know the Copart system and bring appropriate documentation.

Export & Dealer Coordination

Many Copart buyers are export dealers shipping vehicles to international destinations. We handle the U.S.-side transport to ports, container yards, and consolidators with appropriate documentation for export.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can Heartland dispatch a copart auto transport shipment?

For most routes, we can dispatch a carrier within 1-3 business days of booking. Time-critical situations (hot shot, expedited) can dispatch same-day depending on origin and current carrier availability. Standard shipments dispatch on a 3-5 day pickup window with the carrier confirming a specific arrival 24 hours before pickup.

What does copart auto transport cost?

Pricing depends on distance, vehicle or load specifications, route popularity, time of year, and service urgency. Standard shipments range from a few hundred dollars for short routes to $1,500-$2,500+ for cross-country. Specialty services (enclosed, expedited, hot shot, oversize) cost more but the premium reflects real operational differences. Every Heartland quote is transparent and all-in — no fuel surcharges, no rate increases at delivery, no hidden fees.

Is my shipment insured?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, with coverage limits typically $100,000-$250,000 per vehicle for standard auto transport. Specialty cargo (commercial freight, oversize loads, high-value vehicles) often requires supplemental coverage which we arrange and document at the quote stage. Insurance covers transit-related damage from pickup through delivery.
