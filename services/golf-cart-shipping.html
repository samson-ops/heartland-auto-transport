Golf Cart Shipping — Smaller Vehicles, Specific Needs

Golf carts and low-speed vehicles (LSVs) might be small, but transporting them requires the right setup. Golf carts have no road registration (typically), no road-legal lighting in most cases, low ground clearance and small tires that don't tolerate ramps well, and often lithium battery packs or lead-acid batteries that need specific transport handling. Custom golf carts and street-legal LSVs add complexity with aftermarket modifications — lifts, oversized tires, custom paint, sound systems, lighting packages, and seat extensions for 4 and 6-passenger configurations.

Heartland Auto Transport handles golf cart shipping with carriers experienced specifically with small low-clearance vehicles. We ship Club Car (Onward, Tempo, Villager, Precedent, DS), E-Z-GO (RXV, TXT, Express, S4, S6, Liberty, 2Five), Yamaha Drive2 (PowerTech AC, EFI, Quietech), Cushman Hauler and Refresher series, ICON Electric Vehicles (i20, i40, i60, i80), Garia luxury golf carts, Tomberlin street legal LSVs, GEM (Global Electric Motorcars) by Polaris, Star EV golf carts, Bintelli street legal carts and LSVs, custom-built golf carts (slammed, lifted, stretched, custom-painted), and vintage and restored golf carts (1980s/90s Club Car and EZ-GO).

Many of our golf cart shipments are between Alabama and Florida — Gulf Coast snowbirds, Villages residents, and beachfront properties drive significant cart shipping demand.

Golf Cart Shipping Methods

Most golf carts ship on open multi-vehicle carriers — the same trailers that haul cars, but with carts secured in specific positions that accommodate their low clearance. For high-end custom carts, vintage restorations, or carts going through extreme weather, enclosed transport is available at a premium. Trailer transport works for carts with their own road trailers — relatively rare. Most golf carts don't have legal road trailers because they don't need them in their normal use. Hauled transport is most common. The cart is loaded onto an open carrier or enclosed trailer with proper securement designed for small, low-clearance vehicles. Multiple carts can ship together at significant per-cart discounts.

Preparing Your Golf Cart for Transport

  • Charge the battery to about 50% — don't ship with a fully drained battery (which could die during transit) or fully charged (some carriers prefer mid-charge for safety).
  • Disconnect the battery using the cutoff switch or terminal disconnect.
  • Remove or secure all loose accessories: coolers, club holders, cup holders, removable seats, sound system components.
  • Lock the steering wheel if your cart has that feature.
  • Document the condition with detailed photos: body panels, seats, dash, accessories, undercarriage.
  • Provide the key or fob — drivers need to move the cart on and off the carrier.
  • Note any modifications: lifts, oversized tires, custom paint, electrical accessories. Affects tie-down points.
  • Verify tire pressure is appropriate. Properly inflated tires are necessary for loading.
  • Disconnect any aftermarket alarm systems that could trigger during transit.
  • If the cart has a charger: remove and ship separately or secure it inside the cart.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does golf cart shipping cost?

Golf cart shipping from Alabama typically costs $300 to $1,300 depending on distance and route. Multi-cart shipments (2+ carts together) get significant per-cart discounts. Alabama-to-Florida is one of our most common and affordable cart routes.

Can you ship a custom lifted golf cart with oversized tires?

Yes. Custom carts with lifts, oversized tires, and aftermarket accessories ship regularly. Tell us the modifications when you book so we match you with a carrier whose trailer accommodates the cart's actual dimensions.

Do you ship golf carts with lithium battery packs?

Yes, but lithium-powered carts (like newer EZ-GO Liberty Lithium, ICON i-Series, and Club Car Tempo Lithium) require carriers with appropriate battery transport authorization. We handle the carrier matching.

Can you deliver to The Villages, Sun City, or other cart-friendly communities?

Absolutely. We regularly deliver carts to The Villages FL, Sun City AZ, and dozens of other cart-friendly retirement communities and resorts. We coordinate with community gates and HOA management for delivery access.

Should I ship my high-end custom cart open or enclosed?

For high-end custom builds (Garia, custom ICON, custom-painted carts worth $20,000+), enclosed transport is worth the premium for protection from weather and road debris. Standard carts ship safely on open carriers.

Can you ship multiple carts together to save money?

Yes. Multi-cart shipments to the same destination receive significant per-cart discounts. Common for snowbird couples shipping his/hers carts, families with multiple residences, or community groups coordinating shipments.
