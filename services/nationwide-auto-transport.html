Alaska Service

Alaska shipments are a specialty. Vehicles ship via standard carrier to Tacoma, Washington, then transfer to Alaska-specific carriers for the final route up the Alaska Highway or via Marine Highway ferry. Timeline: typically 2-4 weeks total. Pricing: significantly higher than continental shipments due to specialized routing.

Hawaii Service

Hawaii shipments combine continental U.S. transport with ocean container shipping. Vehicles ship to the Port of Long Beach (West Coast routing) or Port of Jacksonville (East Coast routing), then via container ship to Honolulu, Hilo, Kahului, or Lihue. Timeline: 2-3 weeks total. Pricing requires specialty quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you ship to all 50 states?

Yes. Heartland Auto Transport ships vehicles to and from all 50 U.S. states. The 48 continental states are handled with our standard carrier network. Alaska and Hawaii require specialty carriers and longer timelines, but we ship to both regularly.

How long does nationwide auto transport take?

Transit time depends on distance. Short routes (under 500 miles) take 1-3 days. Mid-distance moves (500-1,500 miles) take 3-7 days. Cross-country (1,500+ miles) takes 7-14 days. Alaska and Hawaii take 2-4 weeks due to specialty routing.

How much does nationwide car shipping cost?

Continental U.S. shipments range from $400 (short interstate) to $1,800 (true cross-country) for standard vehicles on open transport. Alaska runs $2,500-$4,500. Hawaii runs $1,800-$2,800 including ocean container.

Can you ship cars internationally?

We focus on U.S. domestic shipping. For Canada, Mexico, or overseas, we can refer you to international auto shipping specialists with the customs and port expertise required. Domestic U.S. (all 50 states) is our core service.

How do nationwide shipments to Alaska or Hawaii work?

Alaska shipments go via standard carrier to Tacoma, WA, then specialty Alaska carrier to your destination. Hawaii shipments go via container ship from Long Beach or Jacksonville ports. Both require specialty pricing and 2-4 week timelines.

Is nationwide service more expensive than regional service?

Not really — pricing is based on distance, not whether the shipment is regional or nationwide. A 500-mile shipment costs the same whether it's regional or interstate. What changes pricing is the total distance, route, vehicle size, and transport type.
