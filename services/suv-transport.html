Why SUV Transport Requires Specialized Handling

SUV transport isn't quite the same as shipping a sedan. SUVs come in a huge range of sizes — from sub-compact crossovers like the Toyota Corolla Cross or Honda HR-V, all the way up to massive three-row vehicles like the Ford Expedition Max, GMC Yukon XL, or Chevrolet Suburban. The largest full-size SUVs can be over 18 feet long and weigh nearly three tons, which affects how they're loaded on the carrier and what slots they occupy.

At Heartland Auto Transport, we match each SUV to the right carrier configuration. Compact crossovers ship on standard open carriers right alongside sedans. Mid-size SUVs like the Toyota Highlander, Honda Pilot, or Ford Explorer ship the same way without issue. Full-size and oversized SUVs sometimes require carriers with extra clearance, or specific deck positions that accommodate their height and weight. Our dispatchers handle all this matching for you — you just tell us the year, make, and model, and we make sure the right carrier shows up.

Open vs. Enclosed SUV Transport

The vast majority of SUVs ship on open carriers — it's the standard, affordable, and safe option for daily-driver SUVs. However, for high-end luxury SUVs (Range Rover, Mercedes G-Wagon, Cadillac Escalade Platinum, Lincoln Navigator Black Label), classic Broncos or Land Cruisers, or any modified or lifted SUV that's particularly valuable, we recommend our enclosed auto transport service. Enclosed shipping protects the vehicle from weather, road debris, and prying eyes — important for high-value SUVs being relocated to or from Alabama.

What Does SUV Transport Cost from Alabama?

SUV shipping costs slightly more than sedan shipping because SUVs are larger and heavier, occupying more space and weight capacity on the carrier. As a general rule, expect SUV transport to cost 10-20% more than the same route for a compact sedan. A typical Birmingham-to-Atlanta SUV shipment runs $500-$700. Mobile to Houston SUV transport costs around $700-$900. Cross-country Alabama-to-California SUV transport ranges from $1,300 to $1,700 depending on the SUV size and the season. Lifted SUVs, modified off-road builds, and oversized 3-row SUVs may incur a small surcharge.

How to Prepare Your SUV for Transport

  • Wash your SUV before pickup so any pre-existing scratches, dings, or paint chips are clearly visible during inspection.
  • Remove all personal items above the window line. Items below the window line in the cargo area are typically OK up to 100 lbs total.
  • Reduce gas to a quarter tank — full tanks add unnecessary weight.
  • Disable any aftermarket alarms or anti-theft systems that could be triggered during transport.
  • Fold side mirrors in and retract any antenna if possible.
  • Document existing condition with photos from multiple angles, including the roof and undercarriage if accessible.
  • Provide two sets of keys if you have them — one for the driver and one as backup.
  • If your SUV has any mechanical issues (won't start, flat tire, etc.), notify us upfront so we send the right carrier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does SUV transport cost?

SUV transport from Alabama typically costs $500 to $1,700 depending on size, distance, and route. Compact crossovers ship at near-sedan prices. Full-size SUVs cost 10-20% more. Cross-country SUV shipments average $1,200-$1,700.

Can you ship oversized or lifted SUVs?

Yes. We have carriers equipped to handle lifted SUVs, oversized 3-row models, and modified off-road builds. Lifted vehicles may incur a small surcharge because they occupy extra deck height. Let us know your SUV's modifications when you request a quote.

Do you ship hybrid and electric SUVs?

Absolutely. We ship hybrid SUVs like the Toyota Highlander Hybrid and Ford Explorer Hybrid, plus full electric SUVs like the Tesla Model Y, Rivian R1S, Cadillac Lyriq, and Ford Mustang Mach-E. See our EV shipping page for EV-specific considerations.

Should I choose open or enclosed transport for my SUV?

Open transport is fine for daily-driver SUVs. Choose enclosed transport for luxury SUVs (Range Rover, G-Wagon, Escalade), classic restored Broncos or Land Cruisers, or any SUV worth $80,000+ where extra protection is worth the cost.

Can I put items in my SUV's cargo area during transport?

Most carriers allow up to 100 lbs of personal items in the cargo area below the window line. Items aren't covered by cargo insurance, so avoid shipping irreplaceables. Never ship firearms, hazardous materials, or anything that can shift during transit.
