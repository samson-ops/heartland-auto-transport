Auto Transport Services We Run From Montgomery

We offer the full menu for Montgomery customers. Every load is carried by an FMCSA-licensed carrier, fully insured in transit, and quoted door to door with the price locked. No fuel surcharge bolted on later, no "the rate went up" call before delivery.

Open Transport

This is what most people use, and for good reason. Your car rides on a standard multi-car open trailer, the same kind that drops fresh inventory at dealerships along the Eastern Boulevard all week. It's the right call for daily drivers, family SUVs, pickups, and almost anything you bought used or online from an out-of-state seller. Open auto transport covers the large majority of what we ship and costs noticeably less than enclosed.

Enclosed Transport

If the vehicle is worth protecting from weather and road grime, a low-mileage luxury car, an exotic, a restored classic, anything with paint you don't want chipped, go with enclosed transport. It rides inside a sealed trailer, away from the elements. Plan on roughly 60 to 80 percent more than open. For collector and show vehicles, our classic car transport service uses carriers who do this for a living. Not sure which way to go? Our breakdown of open vs. enclosed transport lays out the trade-offs.

Door-to-Door Service

We pick the vehicle up at your Montgomery address, whether that's a house, apartment, office, or dealer lot, and deliver it to the address you give us. When a full-size carrier physically can't get down a tight street or into a gated complex, the driver arranges to meet you a few blocks away at a wide spot like a shopping-center lot. That's normal practice everywhere, not a Montgomery quirk.

Expedited Transport

Tight deadline? Expedited service moves your shipment to the front of the line. It runs about 20 to 40 percent over standard, but it tightens the pickup window from a few days down to 24 to 48 hours and usually trims a day or two off transit. A report date that won't move, a closing across the country, a sudden job start, that's when people reach for it.

How Shipping a Car From Montgomery Works

1. Get your quote

Call (334) 326-1484 or use the quote tool on this page. Tell us the pickup spot in Montgomery, the delivery address, the year, make, and model, and roughly when you'd like it collected. You'll have an all-in number within a few minutes, with no email wall, no pressure tactics, and no fees that surface later.

2. Book it

Accept the quote and we set the order with a small deposit, usually $100 to $200 and never more. The balance goes to the driver at delivery, by cashier's check or cash. From there, a dedicated dispatcher owns your shipment start to finish.

3. Pickup at your address

The carrier calls about a day ahead to lock a window. The driver shows up in Montgomery, walks the vehicle with you, and fills out the Bill of Lading noting its condition. You both sign, it's loaded, and you're underway. A little prep beforehand makes this smoother, our 10-step prep checklist covers it.

4. Updates in transit

You keep a direct line to your dispatcher and can call whenever. Timing tracks distance: short regional hops out of Montgomery run 1 to 3 days, mid-distance moves 3 to 5, and a true coast-to-coast haul 7 to 10.

5. Delivery

The driver calls ahead before reaching the destination. You, or anyone 18 or older you name, meet the truck, look the vehicle over against the pickup paperwork, and sign off. You pay the balance, and that's it.

What Auto Transport Costs From Montgomery

Six things mostly decide the price: distance, vehicle size, how busy the route is, the season, how flexible your dates are, and whether the car runs. Because Montgomery sits on the I-65 and I-85 crossing, carrier supply here is generally healthy, which keeps the common lanes competitive.

Rough expectations for a standard vehicle leaving Montgomery:

  • Under 500 miles: about $350-$700
  • 500 to 1,500 miles: about $700-$1,300
  • Over 1,500 miles: about $1,100-$1,800 open, $2,000+ enclosed

January and February are the cheapest stretch, when capacity is high and demand is low. May through September is the priciest, with summer moves, college changeovers, and family relocations all hitting together. Booking three to six weeks out usually lands the best rate; waiting until the last ten days can cost 25 to 40 percent more, because the carrier knows you're boxed in. Montgomery Auto Transport FAQ

How much does it cost to ship a car from Montgomery?

It comes down to distance and the vehicle. As a rough guide, short routes out of Montgomery run about $350-$700, mid-distance moves $700-$1,300, and cross-country shipments $1,100-$1,800 on an open carrier. Enclosed adds roughly 60 to 80 percent. The route list above gives specific Montgomery lanes, and a quick quote will pin down your exact number.

How long does it take?

Start to finish, most shipments land in 5 to 15 days. Pickup usually happens 1 to 5 days after you book, and transit depends on distance: 1 to 3 days for in-state and neighboring states, 3 to 5 for Texas, Florida, or the Carolinas, and 7 to 10 for the West Coast and Pacific Northwest.

Do you really pick up at my door in Montgomery?

Yes. We collect at your specific Montgomery address and deliver to the one you give us. If a big rig genuinely can't fit, a narrow street, a low parking deck, a gated community with size limits, the driver meets you at a nearby spot with room to load, usually within a mile.

Is my vehicle insured while it's being shipped?

Yes. Every carrier in our network carries active FMCSA-required cargo insurance, typically $100,000 to $250,000 per vehicle on standard transport, with higher limits available through our enclosed network for high-value cars. Coverage applies to damage in transit, from pickup to delivery; anything already noted on the Bill of Lading at pickup isn't covered.

Can you ship a car that doesn't run?

Yes, as long as it rolls, steers, and brakes. An inoperable vehicle needs a winch-equipped carrier and usually runs a $100 to $200 surcharge, just flag it when you book so we send the right truck. Project cars, recent auction buys, and inherited vehicles are common ones we move this way.

How far ahead should I book?

Three to six weeks is the sweet spot for price and scheduling. Inside ten days you'll typically pay 25 to 40 percent more, and summer plus the holiday weeks, Thanksgiving through New Year, book up fast, so give those even more lead time.

How do I get the car ready?

Wash it so the condition photos are clear, snap pictures from every angle, leave about a quarter tank of fuel, clear out personal items above the window line, switch off any aftermarket alarm, and hand the driver two sets of keys. The full rundown is in our vehicle prep guide.
