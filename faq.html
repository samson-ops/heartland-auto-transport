💰 Pricing & Cost

How much does auto transport cost?

Auto transport from Alabama typically costs $500 to $1,800 for standard vehicles depending on distance, vehicle size, and route. Short regional routes (Alabama to Georgia, Florida, Tennessee) start at $400-$700. Mid-distance shipments (Alabama to Texas, Carolinas, Virginia) run $700-$1,200. Cross-country shipments (Alabama to California, Northeast, Pacific Northwest) range $1,000-$1,800. Luxury and exotic vehicles in enclosed transport cost more. Get a free quote for accurate pricing on your route.

What factors affect auto transport cost?

Six main factors: distance (longer trips cost more total but less per mile), vehicle size and weight (full-size SUVs and pickup trucks cost 10-25% more than compact cars), route popularity (major corridors like I-65, I-10, I-20 are cheaper than remote pickups), time of year (summer is peak season; January and February are cheapest), pickup flexibility (5-7 day windows get the best rates), and transport type (open is cheapest, enclosed costs 60-80% more).

Why are auto transport quotes so different from company to company?

Because the auto transport industry has more brokers than honest pricing. Some companies lure customers with extremely low quotes they have no intention of honoring — they book the shipment, then "discover" the price needs to increase, often after you've waited days for pickup. Heartland's quotes are all-in: the price you accept is the price you pay. We'd rather lose a quote on honest pricing than win one we'll have to renegotiate.

How do I pay for auto transport?

Most shipments require a small deposit at booking (typically $100-$200, applied toward your total). The remaining balance is collected on delivery, usually by cashier's check or cash to the driver. Some shipments can be prepaid in full by credit card upfront. We discuss payment options when you book — there's no single required method.

Are there hidden fees I should know about?

Not with Heartland. Our quotes include fuel costs, insurance, door-to-door pickup and delivery, and standard handling. The only situations where additional fees apply are: non-running vehicles (typically $100-$200 surcharge), oversized vehicles requiring specialty trailers, and last-minute date changes. We disclose any potential additional fees upfront — never at delivery.

⏰ Timing & Transit

How long does auto transport take?

Transit times depend on distance: In-state Alabama: 1-3 days. Regional (Alabama to GA, FL, TN, MS): 2-5 days. Mid-distance (Alabama to TX, NC, VA, OH): 3-6 days. Cross-country (Alabama to CA, WA, NY, ME): 7-10 days. Pickup typically happens 1-5 days after booking. Total time from booking to delivery is usually 3-15 days.

How quickly can you pick up my vehicle?

Pickup windows are typically 1-5 days from booking. If you need faster pickup — same day or next day — our expedited auto transport service can usually accommodate, at premium pricing. Guaranteed-date pickup is also available for time-sensitive shipments.

Can you guarantee an exact pickup or delivery date?

Standard transport provides estimated windows rather than guaranteed dates. For shipments requiring specific dates (military PCS moves, closing dates on home sales, time-sensitive auctions), our guaranteed pickup service locks in your exact date for a premium. Delivery dates can also be guaranteed in some cases.

What if my pickup date changes?

Life happens — dates change. As long as you give us 24+ hours notice, date changes are usually accommodated without fees. Same-day or last-minute changes may incur a small rescheduling fee depending on whether the carrier has already dispatched. Call your dispatcher as soon as you know about any changes.

📋 The Process

How does the auto transport process work?

5 steps: (1) Request a free quote, (2) Book your shipment with a small deposit, (3) Driver picks up your vehicle from your address, (4) Vehicle is transported with regular updates from your dispatcher, (5) Driver delivers to your destination, you inspect, sign, and the keys are yours. See our complete How It Works guide for full details.

Do you offer door-to-door service?

Yes. Our drivers come to your home, business, or any Alabama address — and deliver to any address nationwide. There's no driving to terminals or waiting at freight depots. If a tractor-trailer can't legally access your street (low clearance, narrow turn-around, gated community restrictions), we coordinate a nearby meet-up location like a large parking lot or rest area.

Do I need to be present for pickup and delivery?

Either you or a designated representative (18+ with photo ID) must be present at both pickup and delivery to inspect the vehicle and sign the Bill of Lading. The representative can be a family member, friend, neighbor, or anyone you trust. If you can't be present at all, talk to us about alternatives.

What is a Bill of Lading?

The Bill of Lading is the legal document recording your vehicle's condition at pickup and delivery. At pickup, you and the driver inspect the vehicle together and document any existing damage, dings, or scratches. At delivery, you do the same and confirm the vehicle arrived in the same condition. Both copies are signed. This document is your protection if any damage occurs.

How do I track my shipment?

Your dedicated dispatcher provides regular updates throughout transit. You can call, text, or email anytime for status — there's no rotating call center queue. For cross-country shipments, you typically receive 2-3 updates per day. Real-time GPS tracking is available on most shipments through your dispatcher.

🛡️ Insurance & Safety

Is my vehicle insured during transport?

Yes. Every carrier in our network maintains active cargo insurance covering your vehicle from pickup to delivery. Standard coverage is adequate for typical passenger vehicles. For high-value vehicles (luxury cars over $80,000, exotics, classics worth $50,000+), supplemental coverage is available on request — typically with agreed-value policies for $250,000-$1,000,000+ coverage.

What if my vehicle is damaged during transport?

Damage during transport is rare with vetted carriers — well under 1% of shipments — but if anything happens, you're protected. Document the damage on the Bill of Lading at delivery, take photos, and contact us immediately. Your dispatcher walks you through the claim process with the carrier's cargo insurance company. We don't disappear after delivery — we stay involved until any issue is fully resolved.

Are Heartland's carriers properly licensed?

Yes. Every carrier in our network is verified for: active FMCSA authority (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration), current cargo insurance with adequate coverage, a clean safety record (we check FMCSA SAFER database), and verified customer feedback. We don't dispatch your vehicle to cheap, uninsured carriers that cut corners.

What is FMCSA and why does it matter?

The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) is the federal agency that licenses and regulates auto transport carriers and brokers. Any legitimate auto transport company holds FMCSA authority, which can be verified at safer.fmcsa.dot.gov. Heartland Auto Transport is an FMCSA Licensed Broker. Always verify FMCSA status before booking with any auto transport company.

🚗 Vehicle Types

What types of vehicles can you ship?

Everything: cars, SUVs, pickup trucks (including lifted and dually), motorcycles, electric vehicles (Tesla, Rivian, Lucid, Ford Lightning), luxury cars, exotic supercars, classic cars, antique vehicles, RVs, boats, ATVs/UTVs, golf carts, vans, and heavy equipment.

What is the difference between open and enclosed auto transport?

Open auto transport uses standard multi-car carriers exposed to weather and road debris. It's the most affordable and common option, used to ship roughly 95% of vehicles including new cars to dealerships. Enclosed transport ships your vehicle inside a fully covered trailer, recommended for classic, exotic, luxury, or high-value vehicles. Open transport costs 30-60% less than enclosed.

Can you ship a non-running vehicle?

Yes — we ship inoperable vehicles, but they require a carrier with a winch. There's typically a $100-$200 surcharge for non-running vehicles. The vehicle needs to roll, steer, and brake — if any of these don't work, tell us upfront so we send the right equipment.

Can you ship a lifted truck or modified vehicle?

Yes. We have carriers equipped for lifted trucks with up to 8-inch lifts and oversized tires. Tell us the total truck height and tire size so we match you with a carrier that has proper overhead clearance. Modified vehicles, dually trucks, and oversized SUVs ship regularly.

Do you ship to and from auctions and dealerships?

Absolutely. We regularly pick up from Manheim, Copart, IAA, dealer auctions, and individual dealerships. We also deliver to dealers, auction houses (Mecum, Barrett-Jackson, RM Sotheby's), restoration shops, and storage facilities. Tell us the pickup or delivery location's specific access requirements.

📦 Vehicle Preparation

How do I prepare my vehicle for transport?

Wash your vehicle so pre-existing damage is clearly visible. Reduce gas to a quarter tank. Remove all personal items above the window line. Disable aftermarket alarms. Fold mirrors in. Provide a key (and ideally a backup). Document the condition with photos from multiple angles. Note any mechanical issues upfront — non-running vehicles need a winch-equipped carrier.

Can I put personal items in my vehicle during shipping?

Most carriers allow up to 100 pounds of personal items placed in the trunk below the window line. Items are not covered by cargo insurance, so don't ship anything irreplaceable. Never ship firearms, explosives, hazardous materials, perishables, or living things. Items above the window line can shift during transit and are typically not permitted.

Should my gas tank be full or empty?

Keep your gas tank at about a quarter full. Less fuel reduces total carrier weight (which matters for fuel costs and DOT weight limits) and reduces fire risk during transit. Don't drain it completely — the driver needs to be able to move the vehicle on and off the trailer.

Should I keep my keys in the vehicle?

Provide one set of keys to the driver at pickup. They need to be able to load and unload your vehicle. If you have a second set, keep it as backup. After delivery, the keys come back to you. Never give the driver your only set of keys — always keep a spare with you or trusted family.
